🎣 What is Phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering attack where cybercriminals impersonate trusted entities — banks, tech companies, or colleagues — to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

Phishing is responsible for 90% of data breaches worldwide. It exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.

📧
Email Phishing Fake emails mimicking banks, services, or coworkers
📱
Smishing Phishing via SMS text messages with malicious links
📞
Vishing Voice phishing — fake calls from "IT support" or "banks"
🎯
Spear Phishing Highly targeted attacks using personal information

Module 3

🧠 Social Engineering Tactics

Attackers exploit psychological triggers to bypass your rational thinking. Here's what to watch for:

  • ⏰
    Urgency & Scarcity — "Your account will be deleted in 24 hours!" Forces rushed decisions without verification.
  • 😨
    Fear & Intimidation — "Legal action will be taken if you don't respond." Creates panic so victims comply without thinking.
  • 🎁
    Greed & Rewards — "You've won a $500 gift card!" Too-good-to-be-true offers exploit wishful thinking.
  • 👤
    Authority Impersonation — Pretending to be your CEO, IT department, or IRS to gain compliance.
  • 🤝
    Familiarity & Trust — "Hi, I'm John from IT, I helped you last month…" Building false rapport to lower your guard.
  • 📎
    Curiosity Bait — "See who viewed your profile" or unexpected invoice attachments that trigger clicks.
Module 4

🛡️ How to Avoid Becoming a Victim

  • 🔗
    Hover before you click — Always hover over links to preview the real URL in your browser's status bar.
  • 📨
    Verify the sender — Check the full email address, not just the display name. "PayPal Support" could be from attacker@evil.com.
  • 🔐
    Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) — Even if credentials are stolen, MFA blocks unauthorized logins.
  • 🚫
    Never provide passwords over email or phone — Legitimate organizations will never ask for this.
  • 📞
    Call to verify — If in doubt, call the company directly using the official number from their website.
  • 🔄
    Keep software updated — Patches fix vulnerabilities that phishing malware exploits after you click a link.
  • 🚨
    Report suspicious emails — Use the "Report Phishing" button in your email client or forward to your IT team.
